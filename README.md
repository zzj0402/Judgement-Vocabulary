# Judgement-Vocabulary

This repository maintains a curated list of vocabularies and expressions that are categorized as "judgements" which often hinder empathetic and clear communication.

## Purpose

Judgements are often seen as "tragic expressions" of unmet needs. 
By identifying and categorizing binary, moralistic, or non-empathetic language, this project aims to increase awareness of habitual judgmental thinking.

## Structure

Language files current available in TOON and JSON format: each file contains a list of vocabularies and expressions following the following schema:

### Spelling
Sorted alphabetically.

### Categories

- **Moralistic Judgements (`MJ`)**: Words and phrases that imply moral judgements, including blame, insults, labels, criticism, and diagnoses (right/wrong, good/bad).
- **Comparisons (`COMP`)**: Words and phrases that block compassion by comparing oneself or others.
- **Denial of Responsibility (`DR`)**: Words and phrases that obscure personal choice and suggest we are forced to act.
- **Making Demands (`MD`)**: Words and phrases that express desires in a way that implies blame or punishment for non-compliance.
- **Deserve-Oriented Language (`DOL`)**: Words and phrases that imply certain behaviors deserve reward or punishment.
- **Interpretations Disguised as Feelings (`IDF`)**: Words and phrases that describe what we think others are doing to us rather than our actual internal emotional state (e.g., "ignored", "betrayed").

### Explanation
A brief explanation of the word or expression.

### Example
An example of the word or expression in a sentence.
